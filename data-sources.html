Where the data comes from.

Every field PropertySight returns is sourced from a record you can name. No proprietary guesswork. The list below is the full set of providers we draw on, what each one contributes, and whether the source is public or licensed.

BatchData

Licensed partner

Our nationwide property and ownership data partner. BatchData consolidates county recorder and assessor records, mortgage, lien, foreclosure, permit, listing, and contact data from thousands of jurisdictions into a normalized feed. The majority of fields PropertySight returns on a scan — and every record purchased through a tier — originates with BatchData. Skip-traced phone numbers are also checked against the FTC's National Do Not Call Registry through this partnership before any contact appears in the app.

  • Owner of record
  • Deed history
  • Last sale price & date
  • Tax assessment
  • Estimated value
  • Equity & loan balance
  • Liens & NOD records
  • Code violations
  • Permit history
  • Recent sales comparables
  • Active & pending listings
  • Owner contact (skip trace)
  • DNC / TCPA flag

Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)

Public data

Mortgage rates, lending indices, and macro indicators that feed into equity calculations and value estimates. Refreshed against the official FRED series so your equity numbers reflect current conditions, not last quarter's.

  • 30-year fixed mortgage rate
  • Regional housing indices
  • Loan-to-value inputs

Google Maps Platform & Google Solar API

Licensed

Address geocoding, reverse geocoding, satellite imagery, building footprints, and place data — plus the Google Solar API for roof orientation, surface area, and annual sunlight data. When you point your phone at a building, Google's geocoder is what turns your GPS, bearing, and image into a candidate address for confirmation.

  • Address geocoding
  • Reverse geocoding from GPS
  • Satellite imagery
  • Building footprints
  • Roof orientation & area
  • Annual sunlight (kWh / m²)

Refunded if unverified.

When a tier is purchased and the underlying record cannot be confirmed, your credit is returned automatically — with a note on which source came up empty. The data is only as good as the records behind it, and we will not pretend otherwise.
